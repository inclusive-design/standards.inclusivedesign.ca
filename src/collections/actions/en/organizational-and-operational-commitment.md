longterm: |-
  - Make inclusion part of the organization’s mission, internal policies, and public communications.
  - Provide dedicated funding and staff support for accessible and inclusive outreach and participation.
  - Define diversity, equity, and inclusion goals clearly and treat them as ongoing commitments, not checkboxes.
  - Align policies and funding priorities with equity and inclusion goals.

moreInformation: |-
  - [Functional accessibility requirement](https://accessible.canada.ca/creating-accessibility-standards/canasc-en-301-5492024-accessibility-requirements-ict-products-and-services/4-functional-performance) from Accessibility Standards Canada treats accessibility as something that must be built into the standard itself, not added later. It shows that inclusion should be part of the technical requirement and the organization’s ongoing work.
  - [Ladder of citizen participation by Co-designing Inclusive Cities](https://cities.inclusivedesign.ca/resources/ladder-of-citizen-participation/) explains that organizations should move beyond one-off consultation and give people real influence. That supports a stronger organizational commitment because it means participation is built into the process, not treated as an extra step.
  - [Build lasting relationships by Co-designing Inclusive Cities](https://cities.inclusivedesign.ca/resources/build-lasting-relationships/)[ ](https://cities.inclusivedesign.ca/resources/build-lasting-relationships/)says inclusion depends on trust, honesty, clear roles, and long-term relationships with collaborators. That reflects operational commitment because the organization has to support participation with time, resources, and respectful engagement.
  - [Enable continuous improvement by Co-designing Inclusive Cities](https://cities.inclusivedesign.ca/resources/enable-continuous-improvement/) points to learning from feedback over time and adjusting the work as needed. That matches the idea of making inclusion part of the organization’s internal practice, not just its public message.
