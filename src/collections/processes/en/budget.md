## Summary?

Budgeting involves planning and accounting for access needs from the start. This includes compensation, accessibility support, technology, travel, and any other costs needed for full participation.

## Why is this important?

Budget is important because accessibility and inclusion need to be planned and funded from the start. It helps pay for things like interpreters, captions, accessible documents, technology, travel, and compensation. This is especially important because many Disabled people have limited financial resources. Without enough funding, access needs may be missed, making it harder for people to take part in the standards development process. 

Without enough budget, access needs can be missed or added too late, which makes the process harder for D/deaf and D/disabled people and can leave important voices out.
