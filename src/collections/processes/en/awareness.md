## Summary

Inclusive public awareness involves clearly sharing information about the draft standard and how to take part. Information needs to be easy to find, easy to understand, and shared in accessible formats so D/deaf and D/disabled people know about the process and how to give feedback.

## Why is this important?

Public awareness matters in standards development because people cannot take part in a process they do not know exists. When people understand what standards are, how they are developed, and how they differ from laws, they are more likely to trust the process and join it early.

It also helps people understand that standards can affect everyday life, from products and services to accessibility and safety. If the public does not know they can participate, the process may miss important community voices, especially from people who are most affected by the standard.
