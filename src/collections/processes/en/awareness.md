## Summary

Inclusive public awareness involves clearly sharing information about standards, how they are developed and how to take part in standards development. Information needs to be easy to find, easy to understand, and shared in accessible formats so D/deaf and D/disabled people know about the process and how to give feedback.

## Why is this important?

When people understand what standards are and how they affect everyday life, from products and services to accessibility and safety, they are more likely to want to be part of the process of developing and improving standards. 

People cannot take part in a process they do not know exists. If they aren't aware that they can participate, the process may miss important community voices, especially from people who are most affected by the standard.
